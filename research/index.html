Detecting and Preventing Spoofed Sessions

This project explores how passive contextual information can expose IP‚Äëspoofed sessions. By assigning evolving trust scores, incorporating region‚Äëaware rules, and controlling overlap between normal and attacker profiles, we aim to enhance authentication systems without burdening users.

Key Objectives

  • Maintain high classification accuracy (AUC > .9)
  • Keep trust score overlap between normal users and attackers under 5%
  • Ensure new users begin with low trust and build gradually
  • Apply automatic low trust scores to overseas sessions

Recent Progress

We shifted to a more deterministic, pattern‚Äëbased dataset to eliminate unrealistic randomness. This improves interpretability and helps the model separate attackers from legitimate users more reliably. Early evaluations have achieved an AUC of 0.93, meeting our initial performance goal.

How Location Awareness Works

Our model incorporates geographic regions to identify when a session originates outside the U.S. Overseas logins are automatically capped at a low trust score, ensuring that potential spoofed or high‚Äërisk sessions cannot reach high‚Äëtrust levels.
